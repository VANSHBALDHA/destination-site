What to know before visiting Oaxaca City

Oaxaca City is a vibrant colonial city located in the heart of southern Mexico. It’s known for its rich cultural heritage, colorful markets, and mouthwatering cuisine.

Visitors can explore the stunning Oaxaca Cathedral, stroll through the historical zócalo (main square), or take in the scenic views of the surrounding mountains. Don’t miss the chance to visit the ancient Zapotec ruins of Monte Albán, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Oaxaca City is also famous for its traditional crafts, including weaving, pottery, and black clay pottery. The city’s food scene is equally impressive, with famous dishes like mole, tlayudas, and mezcal. Oaxaca City offers a perfect blend of culture, history, and culinary delights for travelers seeking both relaxation and exploration.

Top sights in Oaxaca City

These popular destinations have a lot to offer

Monte Albán

Discover the ancient Zapotec city of Monte Albán, perched on a mountain with stunning views of Oaxaca Valley.

Templo de Santo Domingo

Visit the beautiful Templo de Santo Domingo, a Baroque church with stunning architecture and history.

Zócalo de Oaxaca

The heart of the city, the Zócalo is a lively square where you can enjoy local music, food, and culture.

Huatulco Beaches

Relax on the beautiful beaches of Huatulco, known for its nine bays and crystal-clear waters.

Top 10 Things to See and Do in Oaxaca City

1. Visit El Castillo in the Mayan Architectural Zone

2. Head to the beautiful beaches: Playa Ruinas, Santa Fe, Playa Paraiso, South Beach, or Middle Beach

3. Visit some of the famous cenotes: El Gran Cenote, Cenote dos Ojos, and Cenote Narahon

4. Explore the ecological parks such as Sian Ka’an Biological reserve and Punta Laguna nature preserve

5. Indulge in local delicacies by trying out the street foods, national dishes, and local favorites

6. Sip some Mezcal and tequila at one of the many beach sidebars

7. Go on a cultural excursion and take in some of the local art, architecture, and culture Oaxaca City has to offer

8. Support local merchants by checking out many of the locally owned shops

9. Go on a bike trip and explore nearby sceneries

10. Take your photo in front of the famous Oaxaca City sign
